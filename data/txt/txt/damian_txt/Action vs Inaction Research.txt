K.K.K 
The Ku Klux Klan is a domestic terrorist organization that was founded shortly after the US civil war ended. The KKK is the oldest and most infamous American hate group. They have used intimidation, violence and murder to maintain white supremacy. Their goals are to repress the rights and freedom of African Americans. They target African Americans, Jews, Latinos, Asian Americans, Native Americans and much more. The first klan used terrorism, physically assaulting and murdering black people. The third klan used murders and bombing to achieve their goals. The Ku Klux Klan aims to “purify” American society. The consequences of their action have been racism, violence and discrimination towards minority groups, especially African Americans. This movement has killed thousands of negros and has lasted over 150 years.

Martin luther king jr (1929-1968) 
Martin Luther King Jr was one of the most prominent leaders of the civil rights movement. He peacefully protested, participated and led marches for the right to vote, desegregation and labor rights. He fought against racial inequality and made the famous I have a dream speech. In this speech, he talks about discrimination against negros. He reminds his people not to be violent in the pursuit of justice. He talks about his dream for a world without racism or discrimination, but with freedom and equality. He inspired many to fight for equality and if he did not take action, segregation, racism and discrimination would continue back then. He helped contribute to the passage of the civil rights act of 1964 and the voting rights act of 1965. 

Adolf Hitler (1889-1945)
Adolf Hitler was the leader of the Nazi party, that aimed to exterminate certain ethnicities they viewed as lesser, especially Jews. Hitler established a totalitarian regime called the third reich. Hitler was crucial to the holocaust: the genocide of over 6 million Jews and millions of others. He held great power and was the leading force of Germany in WW2. He aimed to eliminate Jews from Germany because he viewed them as inferior people and enemies of Germany. He set up various camps used for forced labor, mass murder and permanent incarceration. The consequences of his actions were the extermination of Jews, World War 2 and the deaths of millions of soldiers and civilians.
